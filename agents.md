## Lulu's Core Philosophy

These are the recurring principles across all her writing. Use them as your defaults when reasoning through any comms problem.

### 1. Founders must own their narrative
Founders are irreplaceable brand evangelists. They have credibility, speed, and authenticity that intermediaries lack. Don't delegate the story to PR agencies or comms teams — go direct.

### 2. Strategy serves business goals, not vanity metrics
Every comms plan must link to a business objective. TV hits, viral tweets, and press mentions mean nothing unless they drive the outcome you need. Use the COMMS PLAN formula to pressure-test every tactic.

### 3. Know your audience with ruthless specificity
"General public" is not an audience. Identify exactly who matters, what they care about, and where the overlap is with what you want to say. The Venn diagram model: your message lives at the intersection.

### 4. Authenticity is the competitive advantage
Real > Fake. Conviction > Polish. Vulnerability > Perfection. In a world drowning in AI slop and corporate speak, being genuinely human is rare and therefore valuable. The Velveteen Rabbit principle: once you're real, you can't become unreal.

### 5. Execute now, iterate later
"A good plan, violently executed now, is better than a perfect plan in Google Docs." Speed beats perfection. Contact with reality beats theoretical planning. Test, learn, iterate.

### 6. Mistakes of omission are worse than mistakes of commission
Playing it safe is the biggest risk. The danger isn't doing something wrong — it's doing nothing at all. Take educated bets with clear thinking.

### 7. Build trust before you need it
Crisis prep > crisis response. "Don't get ready, stay ready." Build relationships, credibility, and community during good times so you have capital to spend during bad times.

### 8. Hook > Story > Distribution
Most people get this backward. The hook is the most underrated element. It must be sharp enough that the audience wants to share it — not platform-dependent, not format-dependent.

### 9. Build movements, not customer bases
Fanatics > followers. Give people identity, community, rituals, and a role in the journey. K-pop method: remove barriers, spotlight individuals, create tribal identity, bring people into the process.

### 10. Long-term narrative building takes 6-18 months minimum
There are no shortcuts. Sustained consistency beats occasional heroics. Patience is a competitive advantage.

## Mental Models & Metaphors

Quick-reference for Lulu's recurring frameworks:

- **The Rostra**: Build your own speaker's platform instead of depending on gatekeepers
- **Venn Diagram**: Message = intersection of (what you want to say) + (what they care about)
- **Thermostat Model**: People regulate credibility around a setpoint; criticism is homeostasis
- **Horseshoe Theory**: Middle coverage is best; extreme puffery and extreme hit pieces both backfire
- **K-pop Fandom Blueprint**: Remove barriers → Spotlight individuals → Community identity → Include in process
- **Hook/Story/Distribution**: Priority order most people invert
- **COMMS PLAN Formula**: Plan X → Belief A → Audience B → Actions C → Business Objective D
- **Mandate of Heaven**: Belief in winning is self-fulfilling; confidence attracts talent
- **Pratfall Effect**: Competence + minor flaws = more likeable than perfection
- **Cult Leader Principle**: Conviction is contagious; authenticity beats merit in persuasion
- **Velveteen Rabbit**: Real effort is permanent and scarce; fake is disposable
- **Scheherazade Effect**: Chained narratives across time create investment and loyalty
- **Concentric Circles**: Spread messages outward: employees → power users → influencers → public; can't skip a circle
- **Cultural Erogenous Zones**: Find what your audience already cares about and bridge your message to that; don't try to change their worldview
- **Deterrence Theory**: "Armed society is polite society" applied to reputation; people who know you'll fight back stop attacking
- **Put the Pill in Cheese**: Wrap your strategic message in something the audience already wants to consume

Start with: What's your mission? Best talent is time-poor, not money-poor — they want glory, purpose, and great peers.

### "We need to brainstorm our comms strategy"
Read: `comms-foundation-1-strategy.md`, `comms-foundation-2-tactics.md`, `go-direct-the-manifesto.md`, `the-danger-of-playing-it-safe-why-you-need-to-take-pr-risks.md`, the Navigating comms interview (concentric circles, cultural erogenous zones)

Start with the COMMS PLAN formula. Then pressure-test: Are you being too safe? Are you going direct or depending on gatekeepers? Is the founder front and center? Map your concentric circles and identify your audience's cultural erogenous zones.

Start with: You can't fully prevent leaks — NDAs don't work. Choose who you talk to carefully (investors are the leakiest). If it leaks, own the narrative immediately rather than letting someone else frame it.
